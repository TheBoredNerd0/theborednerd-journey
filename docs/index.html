📚 Lesson: Compound Interest

The Eighth Wonder of the World

Time is the multiplier — start now, even small

📖 Concept

Compound interest: earn returns on your returns. Not just on your original money — on everything you've accumulated. Formula: A = P × (1 + r)ⁿ. A 25-year-old investing $200/month at 8% avg returns has ~$1.1M by 65. Start at 35 with same amounts? ~$487K. Same contribution, 20 extra years doubles the result.

🧠 Key Lesson This Week

Volatility is not the same as risk. A 3% daily crypto drop is just noise if you're investing with a 5–10 year horizon. True risk is: (1) investing money you can't afford to lose, (2) not diversifying, (3) panic-selling. A young investor shouldn't fear a 3% dip — they should see it as a buying opportunity.

🇸🇬 Singapore Context

  • CPF Special Account earns ~4% p.a. — compound interest working for you automatically
  • STI ETF (ES3.SI) has returned ~5–7% annually over long periods — reinvested dividends compound too
  • A 1% fee difference matters enormously over 30 years — a 0.1% fund vs 1.1% fund can cost tens of thousands in foregone growth

📚 Topic: Process Pipelines & xargs Mastery

xargs is the glue that turns linear chains into parallel pipelines. Combined with -P (parallelism) and -0 (null-separated, handles spaces in filenames), you can process thousands of files in seconds.

# Find all files modified in last 7 days, then act on them
find . -mtime -7 -type f | xargs ls -la

# Parallel execution with xargs -P
find . -name "*.log" | xargs -P 4 -I {} sh -c 'gzip {}'

# Count lines in all .md files, sorted
find . -name "*.md" -not -path "./node_modules/*" | xargs wc -l | sort -n

# Find the 5 largest files in a directory
du -sh * | sort -rh | head -5
