📚 Today's Lesson

Dividends: Getting Paid to Hold

The passive income strategy most people overthink

📖 Concept

A dividend is a cash payment a company sends you just for owning their stock. Own 100 shares paying $1/share annual → $100/year doing nothing. Reinvest → compounding. A 5% yield can be a trap if the stock is declining — always check if the company actually earns enough to sustain the payout.

🧠 Key Lesson

Volatility is not the same as risk. A 3% daily crypto drop is just noise if you're investing with a 5–10 year horizon. True risk is: (1) investing money you can't afford to lose, (2) not diversifying, or (3) panic-selling.

🇸🇬 Singapore Angle

Singapore dividends are tax-exempt. Popular payers: Mapletree trusts, CapitaLand Integrated Trust, SingTel — offering 4–6% yields. Open a Syfe/MooMoo/IBKR account, buy a single ETF like CSPX or VWRA, set up a S$20/week DCA plan.
