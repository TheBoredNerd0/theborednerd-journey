# ⚖️ LEGAL CORNER — 2026-05-05

📋 **Today's topic:** AI Content Copyright (Music, Images, Video, Text)

---

🔍 **What you need to know:**

AI-generated content occupies a legal grey zone in 2026. The US Copyright Office generally does not recognise fully AI-generated works as copyrightable — you can *monetise* a Suno track, but you can't *own* it in the traditional sense unless a human has meaningfully transformed the output. The Suno–Warner licensing deal in late 2025 shifted the landscape: free-tier users own nothing commercially; paid subscribers get a commercial *licence* (not ownership), with Suno retaining the "author" status. Meanwhile, the EU AI Act started phasing in through 2024–2026, requiring platforms to disclose AI-generated content — Spotify and Apple Music now enforce DDEX standards and require AI disclosure on upload.

---

✅ **You're fine if you:**

- Use Suno/Udio on a **paid subscription tier** when distributing to Spotify/Apple Music
- **Disclose AI-generated content** via your music distributor (DistroKid, TuneCore, etc.) using the DDEX/Synthetic Content flag — this is now contractually required
- Treat AI-generated music as a **starting point**: re-record vocals, add live instruments, or substantially edit the output to create a human-transformed work you can actually copyright
- Use AI-generated images/video via tools like Pexels (licensed stock footage) or AI image generators **for personal/commercial use** per each platform's terms — always check the specific licence

⚠️ **Watch out for:**

- **Free-tier Suno/Udio output cannot be uploaded to streaming services or monetised YouTube videos** — it's personal use only
- **Copyright claims on AI music are weak** — if someone steals your AI-generated track, your legal standing to sue is very limited unless you've made significant human modifications
- **Mimicking famous artists via AI is risky** — Apple Music's 2026 policy excludes fully AI-tracks from top-tier playlists, and platform strikes for "impersonation" are live
- **EU AI Act obligations** if you're distributing content in Europe — platforms may need to flag AI-generated content under the tiered risk framework now in force
- **Stem/MIDI export before model deprecation** — Suno's terms now cover legacy model outputs, but this could change; lock in your commercial licence while models are still supported

---

🇸🇬 **Singapore note:** Singapore's Copyright Act (currently under review for AI provisions) does not yet have explicit rules on AI-generated work. The government's position leans toward requiring human authorship for protection. No SG-specific AI content labelling law is in force yet, but if you're distributing from Singapore to Spotify/Apple Music, the DDEX disclosure rules still apply as they're platform-imposed requirements, not SG law. IRAS may also scrutinise AI-generated income — keep records of your subscription receipts and distributor payouts for tax purposes.
