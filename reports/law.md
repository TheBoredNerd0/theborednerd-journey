⚖️ LEGAL CORNER — May 4, 2026

📋 Today's topic: AI Content Copyright — Who owns what you create?

🔍 What you need to know:
Generative AI creates a legal grey zone globally. In most jurisdictions (US, EU, UK, SG), AI-generated content lacks copyright protection unless there's significant human creative input — the " Strasbourg test" (humans must be the true author). Stock footage you edit, scripts you write with AI help, and music you produce with AI tools generally qualify; fully automated outputs don't. Platform-level rules (YouTube, Spotify) may further restrict AI-generated content in monetisation.

✅ You're fine if you:
• Use AI as a tool — you write the script, add your voice, make the edits (human authorship is preserved)
• Use royalty-free or licensed stock footage as the visual base for AI voiceover videos
• Add substantial original commentary or narrative structure to AI-generated text
• Use Suno/Udio outputs as background music where you've made meaningful creative decisions (genre, mood, lyrics)

⚠️ Watch out for:
• Uploading fully AI-generated music without adding original arrangement or vocals — Spotify's AI music policy restricts monetisation of purely synthetic tracks
• Using copyrighted artwork as reference for AI image generation — visual style isn't copyrightable, but reproducing specific copyrighted characters/scenes is
• YouTube flagging AI-generated or recycled-content videos for reduced ad rates if the system deems them "repetitive" or "low originality"
• Training data liability — no major ruling yet, but lawsuits against AI companies (Stable Diffusion, OpenAI) are ongoing; using their output commercially carries residual risk

🇸🇬 Singapore note: Singapore's Copyright Act (Cap. 63) protects works where there is a human author. The IPOS position aligns with the WIPO three-step test: creativity must originate from a person. AI-assisted works can be protected if the human makes the key creative choices. There is no specific AI copyright legislation yet, but a 2024 IPOS consultation signaled upcoming guidance.
