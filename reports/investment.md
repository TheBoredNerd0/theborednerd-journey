## 📚 Today's Lesson — Index Funds (Day 2 of Basics)

**What is an index fund?**
Instead of picking individual stocks, you buy a fund that automatically holds a basket of stocks — mirroring an entire market or a segment of it.

The most famous example: the **S&P 500 index fund**. Instead of guessing which of the 500 companies will win, you own all of them. The fund manager doesn't pick and choose — it just holds everything proportional to company size.

**Why it matters:**
• **Low fees:** Because no one is researching stocks to pick, management costs are tiny (often 0.03-0.20% per year vs 1% for active funds)
• **Proven track record:** Over 10-20 year periods, ~80-90% of actively managed funds underperform the index they track
• **Instant diversification:** One purchase gives you exposure to hundreds of companies across sectors

**The math that makes it powerful:**
If the S&P 500 averages ~10% per year (historical long-term average), $1,000 invested today becomes ~$6,700 in 20 years. The compounding effect is quieter than crypto gains but much more reliable.
