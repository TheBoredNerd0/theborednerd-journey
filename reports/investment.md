📚 Learning topic:
**Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)** — This week we're covering DCA, one of the simplest and most effective strategies for building wealth over time. The idea is straightforward: instead of trying to time the market and buy at the "perfect" moment (which even professionals struggle with), you invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals — say S$100 every week — regardless of whether prices are up or down. When prices drop, your S$100 buys more units; when prices rise, you buy fewer. Over time, this smooths out your average purchase price and removes the emotional stress of watching daily swings. Studies consistently show DCA outperforms trying to time the market for most retail investors.

💡 This week's lesson:
**Volatility is not the same as risk** — A 3% daily drop in crypto feels scary, but it's just noise if you're investing with a 5-10 year horizon. True risk is: (1) investing money you can't afford to lose, (2) not diversifying, or (3) selling in a panic. A young investor with decades ahead shouldn't fear a 3% dip — they should see it as a potential buying opportunity. The investors who get hurt are those who over-leverage, panic-sell, or need liquidity they don't have.
